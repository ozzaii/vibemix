## Notes

- Character GLB stays fat because it carries the embedded skin texture + the full
  rigged mesh (Draco position/normal/texcoord quantization shaves ~25% — going
  more aggressive (positionBits 12, normalBits 8) drops it further but starts to
  show on the mesh surface; current setting is the readable-quality sweet spot).
- Animation GLBs collapse from ~28 MB to ~30-185 KB each — the entire payload
  was duplicated mesh + texture data, redundant across all 20 clips. After
  stripping, only the joint hierarchy + sampler input/output accessors remain.
- Two "missing optional extension" warnings on every animation read
  (`KHR_materials_specular`, `KHR_materials_ior`) — these are PBR material
  extensions defined in the source GLB but not in our installed
  `@gltf-transform/core` Extensions module. Safe to ignore: materials are
  removed by the strip step regardless.
- Every animation GLB carries exactly **one** `AnimationClip` after stripping
  (renamed to its canonical CamelCase clip name in `manifest.json`).
- All animations share the same biped skeleton — Three.js
  `SkeletonUtils.retargetClip` will be a no-op normalize at render-load time
  (Plan 13-04).
