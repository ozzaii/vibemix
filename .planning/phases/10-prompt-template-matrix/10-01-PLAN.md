    `vibemix/prompts/matrix.py`: 6 module-level constants (HYPE_BEGINNER, HYPE_INTERMEDIATE, HYPE_PRO, COACH_BEGINNER, COACH_INTERMEDIATE, COACH_PRO). HYPE_INTERMEDIATE is byte-identical to current `vibemix.agent.persona.SYSTEM_INSTRUCTION` for backward compat. The other 5 differentiate by:
    - **Beginner-Hype**: high-energy, casual, fewer technical terms, lots of "yo / damn / sick / vibe / groove". Anchor phrases: "yo that drop", "this groove is sick", "vibe check", "you're cooking", "that switch was clean", "feeling this energy", "dance-floor mood", "this is the moment".
    - **Pro-Hype**: insider DJ vocabulary, terse. Anchor phrases: "that EQ swap landed", "phrase locked", "low-mid pile-up", "32 cleared", "transition was tight", "filter sweep paid off", "stems separated nicely", "build-release timing".
    - **Beginner-Coach**: gentle, encouraging, suggests one specific improvement. "the cut felt early — try 8 bars later", "low boost muddied the breakdown", "give the build more space", "you're rushing the blend".
    - **Intermediate-Coach**: more technical, concrete. "kicks stepped on each other for a half-bar", "EQ killed the lows too aggressively", "build released on the 3 — try the 1", "phrase mismatch in the blend".
    - **Pro-Coach**: peer-level critique, no hand-holding. "phrase ended on the 3", "high-mid pileup at 0:42", "blend overstayed by 16", "transient stack on the kick".

    All 6 share: the negative-dict ban list (inline), describe-before-infer, past-tense, `<silence/>` instruction, KAAN_SPOKE/MANUAL exception (always reply when Kaan asks).
